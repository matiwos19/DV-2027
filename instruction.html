Official instructions for the Diversity Visa Program

DV2027 Instruction

Program Overview

The Department of State administers the Congressionally-mandated Diversity Immigrant Visa Program annually. Section 203(c) of the Immigration and Nationality Act (INA) provides for a class of immigrants known as "diversity immigrants" from countries with historically low rates of immigration to the United States. For fiscal year 2027, 55,000 diversity visas (DVs) will be available.

Note: There is no cost to register for the DV program. The Department of State does not charge a fee for entering the annual DV lottery.

Eligibility Requirements

To enter the DV lottery, you must be a native of an eligible country. Natives of the following countries are not eligible to apply because these countries sent a total of more than 50,000 immigrants to the United States in the previous five years:

  • Bangladesh, Brazil, Canada, China (including mainland and Hong Kong), Colombia, Dominican Republic, El Salvador, Haiti, Honduras, India, Jamaica, Mexico, Nigeria, Pakistan, Philippines, South Korea, United Kingdom (except Northern Ireland) and its dependent territories, and Vietnam.

Additionally, you must meet either the education or work experience requirement:

  • Education: You must have a high school education or its equivalent, defined as successful completion of a 12-year course of formal elementary and secondary education.
  • Work Experience: You must have two years of work experience within the past five years in an occupation that requires at least two years of training or experience to perform.

Application Period

The DV-2027 entry period will run from October 5, 2025, to November 8, 2025. Entries must be submitted electronically during this period. The Department of State will not accept paper entries.

How to Apply

You must submit your entry through the Electronic Diversity Visa (E-DV) website. The entry form will require the following information:

  • Full name
  • Date of birth
  • Gender
  • City of birth
  • Country of birth
  • Photograph (recent, color, passport-style)
  • Mailing address
  • Country of eligibility
  • Education/work experience information
  • Spouse and children information (if applicable)

Important: You may only submit one entry. Individuals who submit more than one entry will be disqualified.

Selection Process

Entries will be selected at random by computer from among all qualified entries. All diversity visas will be allocated among six geographic regions, with a greater number of visas going to regions with lower rates of immigration to the United States.

Beginning May 7, 2026, you will be able to check the status of your entry by returning to the E-DV website and entering your confirmation number. The Department of State will not notify winners by mail or email.

After Selection

If you are selected, you must act promptly. Selected entrants will receive instructions on how to complete their immigrant visa application. You will be required to pay all required visa fees at the time of visa application.

Selected entrants must complete their visa application and receive their visa by September 30, 2027. Diversity visas cannot be issued after this date.

Additional Information

For complete instructions and additional information, please visit the official U.S. Department of State Diversity Visa Program website.

Warning: Beware of fraudulent websites and schemes. The U.S. government will never contact you to request payment for the DV lottery. The only official website for the Diversity Visa Program is the U.S. Department of State's E-DV website.
